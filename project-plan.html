Over the course of 15 weeks, the IT Supervisor Readiness Web Portfolio will be executed through a sequence of systematic phases:

In Week 1, the project will commence with a kickoff phase involving the identification of key objectives, deliverables, and resource selection. Week 2 will be dedicated to strategic planning and content structure, including wireframes for each page (Toolkit, Escalation Matrix, Ticket Response, and Reporting).

During Week 3, the Customer Service Standards Statement will be drafted and finalized, followed by the development of the Escalation Matrix in Week 4. Week 5 will focus on composing a sample Helpdesk Ticket Response document, while Week 6 will address the design of a professional Incident Report Template.

The web interface design begins in Week 7 with the navigation bar layout and user interface flow. Week 8 will include the development of the primary HTML file (index.html) showcasing the toolkit, and in Week 9, the secondary page (project-plan.html) will be built to display the timeline, KPIs, and a financial budget overview.

Aesthetic consistency and responsiveness will be addressed in Week 10 via custom CSS design (style.css). Week 11 will be used for functional validationâ€”testing download links, media responsiveness, and overall user experience across browsers and devices.

In Week 12, all assets including PDFs, DOCX files, and supporting graphics will be compiled. Week 13 will be designated for peer or instructor review, ensuring the content meets quality benchmarks. Following that, Week 14 will involve comprehensive refinements based on the feedback received.

Finally, Week 15 culminates in the formal submission and/or deployment of the full portfolio, ensuring compliance with technical expectations and presentation standards.
